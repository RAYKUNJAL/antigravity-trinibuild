# 🧪 TriniBuild Beta Test Guide
## Early Access Testing Program

---

## 🎯 BETA TEST OBJECTIVES

### Primary Goals
1. **Validate core user flow**: Signup → Store Setup → Product Listing → Payment Setup
2. **Identify friction points**: Where do users get stuck?
3. **Measure completion rates**: How many finish each step?
4. **Gather feature feedback**: What do they love? What's missing?
5. **Test Trinidad-specific features**: COD, local banks, WhatsApp mentions

### Success Metrics
- ✅ **80%+** complete store setup (vs 60-70% industry)
- ✅ **60%+** add at least 1 product (vs 40-50% industry)
- ✅ **50%+** set up payment methods
- ✅ **30%+** go live with their store
- ✅ **5+** qualitative feedback quotes

---

## 👥 BETA TESTER SELECTION

### Ideal Beta Testers (Pick 5-10)
1. **The Established Seller** (currently using Instagram/WhatsApp)
   - Has 50+ products
   - Makes $1-5K/month
   - Wants to look more professional

2. **The Side Hustler** (part-time, evening/weekend business)
   - Has 10-30 products
   - Makes $500-1K/month
   - Wants to scale but limited time

3. **The Brick & Mortar Owner** (physical store, wants online presence)
   - Has inventory in store
   - Mostly walk-in traffic
   - Wants delivery option

4. **The Food Vendor** (doubles, pastries, meal prep)
   - Perishable products
   - Takes orders via WhatsApp
   - COD only

5. **The Service Provider** (photographer, tutor, handyman)
   - Wants to showcase portfolio
   - Needs booking system
   - Payment flexibility

### Where to Find Them
- **Trinidad Buy & Sell Facebook Groups**
- **Local entrepreneur WhatsApp groups**
- **Instagram sellers you follow**
- **Physical stores in your area**
- **Friends/family who sell online**

## 🔍 WHAT TO OBSERVE

### During Onboarding (First 5 Minutes)
- [ ] Do they understand the template options?
- [ ] Do they complete all 3 wizard steps?
- [ ] Do they get confused by any field?
- [ ] Do they see the celebration animation?
- [ ] Do they click "Add First Product"?

### During Product Listing (Next 10 Minutes)
- [ ] Do they choose CSV or Manual?
- [ ] Do they upload images successfully?
- [ ] Do they understand pricing fields (TTD)?
- [ ] Do they add inventory quantities?
- [ ] Do they save the product?

### During Payment Setup (Next 5 Minutes)
- [ ] Do they select COD?
- [ ] Do they configure delivery areas?
- [ ] Do they try to set up cards?
- [ ] Do they understand the fees?

### Overall Experience
- [ ] Do they use mobile or desktop?
- [ ] What device/browser?
- [ ] Where do they hesitate?
- [ ] What do they say out loud?
- [ ] What questions do they ask?

## 🐛 BUG TRACKING

### Common Issues to Watch For

**Critical (Fix Immediately)**:
- [ ] Can't sign up / login
- [ ] Store creation fails
- [ ] Can't upload products
- [ ] Images don't display
- [ ] Payment setup breaks
- [ ] Mobile layout broken

**Important (Fix Within 24h)**:
- [ ] Confusing error messages
- [ ] Slow loading times
- [ ] Missing validation
- [ ] Unclear instructions
- [ ] Bad mobile UX

**Nice to Have (Fix Before Launch)**:
- [ ] Animation glitches
- [ ] Typos
- [ ] Minor UI issues
- [ ] Feature requests

### How to Track
Create a Google Sheet with columns:
- User Name
- Issue Description
- Severity (Critical/Important/Nice)
- Screen/Step Where It Happened
- Device/Browser
- Status (Open/Fixed/Won't Fix)

## 📅 BETA TIMELINE

### Week 1: Onboarding
- **Day 1-2**: Invite testers, send staging link
- **Day 3-5**: Watch them use it, take notes
- **Day 6-7**: Send feedback survey

### Week 2: Iteration
- **Day 8-10**: Fix critical bugs
- **Day 11-12**: Add quick wins from feedback
- **Day 13-14**: Re-test with same users

### Week 3: Validation
- **Day 15-17**: Invite 5 more users (second wave)
- **Day 18-19**: Compare first vs second wave
- **Day 20-21**: Decide: Launch or iterate more?

---

## 🚨 RED FLAGS TO WATCH FOR

**Abandon Beta If**:
- Less than 30% complete store setup (major UX problem)
- Users can't figure out product listing (need redesign)
- No one sets up payment methods (not seeing value)
- Users say "I'll just stick to Instagram" (not compelling enough)

**Iterate If**:
- 50-70% completion (some friction but workable)
- Specific features missing (easy to add)
- Minor confusion points (better copy/tooltips)
- Good feedback but needs polish

**Launch If**:
- 80%+ completion (smooth onboarding)
- Users excited about it ("When can I invite friends?")
- At least 1 sale generated during beta
- Testers willing to pay

## 🎯 POST-BETA DECISIONS

### If It Goes Well
1. Set launch date (2-3 weeks out)
2. Build missing must-have features
3. Polish based on feedback
4. Prepare marketing (social posts, press release)
5. Plan launch promotion

### If It Goes Okay
1. Identify top 3 issues
2. Fix them (1-2 weeks)
3. Do second beta wave
4. Compare results
5. Decide launch or pivot

### If It Goes Poorly
1. Do exit interviews with all testers
2. Identify if it's fixable or fundamental
3. Decide: Pivot product or target market?
4. Don't launch yet - iterate more

## 💡 BETA TEST TIPS

### Do's
- ✅ Watch them use it (screen share or in person)
- ✅ Ask "why?" when they struggle
- ✅ Take notes constantly
- ✅ Thank them profusely
- ✅ Act on feedback quickly

### Don'ts
- ❌ Defend your design choices
- ❌ Explain how to use it (let them struggle - that's the data)
- ❌ Interrupt when they're testing
- ❌ Ignore negative feedback
- ❌ Wait too long to fix critical bugs

---

## 📞 NEED HELP?

If you hit issues during beta:
1. Check error logs in Vercel dashboard
2. Review Supabase logs for database errors
3. Test the exact flow they reported
4. Screenshot the issue
5. Fix and redeploy

**Emergency Rollback**:
If something breaks badly, roll back to previous Vercel deployment in dashboard.
